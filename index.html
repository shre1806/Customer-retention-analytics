Phase 01 · Exploratory analysis

Where churn concentrates

Breaking down the 20.4% churn rate by every customer attribute on file reveals it isn't random — it clusters tightly around a handful of factors.

Churn by geography

Germany churns at 32.4% — roughly double France and Spain.

Churn by age group

The 50–60 band peaks at 56.2% churn; under-40 customers are stable.

Churn by number of products

2-product holders are the most loyal at 7.6%; 3–4 product holders churn at 83–100%.

Churn by activity status

Inactive members churn at 26.9% vs 14.3% for active ones.

Phase 02 · Feature analysis

What actually predicts it

A Random Forest model ranks every attribute by how much it contributes to predicting churn.

Feature importance ranking

Age is the strongest single predictor (24%), followed by salary, credit score, balance, and product count.

Phase 04 · Retention strategy

The action plan

Segment-specific interventions, prioritized by urgency and potential impact.

High risk

Proactive outreachCall Germany + inactive + age 40–60 customers within 30 days (565 customers, 61.2% churn).
Product auditSimplify 3–4 product bundles down to a right-fit 2-product offer.
Retention offersFee waivers / preferential rates for high-balance churners.
Germany campaignInvestigate local competitive pressure directly.

Medium risk

Re-engagementEmail/push campaigns for the 45% currently inactive.
Second-product nudgeUpsell single-product customers toward the 2-product sweet spot.
Financial check-insFree reviews ahead of the age-50 risk inflection point.
Loyalty tiersStatus-based incentives tied to tenure and products.

Low risk

Referral programTurn this loyal, active base into an acquisition channel.
Premium upsellGrow wallet share given lower average balances.
Drift monitoringAuto-alert on falling activity or approaching age 40.

# Phase 4 — Retention Strategy & Action Plan

Translates the Phase 3 risk segments into a concrete, prioritized action plan.

## High risk (3,031 customers · 45.4% churn)

| Priority | Action |
|---|---|
| Critical | Proactive relationship-manager outreach to Germany + inactive + age 40-60 customers within 30 days (565 customers, 61.2% churn) |
| Critical | Product audit for 3-4 product holders; offer simplified, right-fit bundles |
| High | Targeted retention offers (fee waivers, preferential rates) for high-balance churners (avg €109k) |
| High | Germany-specific retention campaign to investigate local competitive pressure |

## Medium risk (5,229 customers · 11.6% churn)

| Priority | Action |
|---|---|
| High | Re-engagement email/push campaign for the 45.4% currently inactive |
| High | Personalized second-product nudge for single-product customers |
| Medium | Financial health check-ins, especially ahead of the age-50 risk inflection point |
| Medium | Loyalty tier introduction based on tenure + products |

## Low risk (1,740 customers · 3.2% churn)

| Priority | Action |
|---|---|
| Medium | Referral program leveraging this loyal, highly active base |
| Medium | Premium product upsell to grow wallet share (avg balance only €24,765) |
| Medium | Automated monitoring for early risk signals (declining activity, approaching age 40) |

## 90-day implementation timeline

| Timing | Action |
|---|---|
| Month 1 | Contact all high-risk customers, prioritizing the Germany + inactive + 40-60 group |
| Month 1-2 | Launch product audit/simplification for 3-4 product holders |
| Month 2 | Roll out re-engagement campaign for inactive medium-risk customers |
| Month 2-3 | Deploy second-product upsell flow |
| Month 3 | Launch referral & loyalty programs |
| Ongoing | Re-score all customers monthly; move customers between segments dynamically |
